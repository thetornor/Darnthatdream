Swingin’
Dream The tune was introduced by the Broadway musical “Swingin’ The Dream”. It was a jazz-infused Broadway adaptation of Shakespeare’s A Midsummer Night’s Dream, featuring an all-star lineup of musicians and a bold attempt to blend jazz with classic theater. Despite its ambition, the show was a commercial failure, closing after just 13 performances.  Concept &
Production Set in New Orleans, rather than ancient Athens, giving the play a jazz-era flavor. Featured a swing music score with contributions from greats like Jimmy Van Heusen, Eddie DeLange, and Benny Goodman. The production was staged at the Center Theatre in New York. Cast &
Music Louis Armstrong played the role of Bottom. Benny Goodman led the orchestra. Maxine Sullivan starred as Titania. Butterfly McQueen known for Gone with the Wind, was also in the cast.
